# Morning Brief — 2026-06-09

## THE ONE THING

OpenAI filed a confidential S-1 with the SEC yesterday — one week to the day after Anthropic's own filing. The headline numbers: $852 billion valuation, Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley as underwriters, 900 million weekly active ChatGPT users, and more than $20 billion in annual recurring revenue. Sam Altman announced the filing pre-emptively: "We expect it to leak so we're just announcing it." The same day, he and chief scientist Jakub Pachocki published a strategic manifesto declaring OpenAI's "third phase" — the economy is now reshaping around AI, and the company's three stated goals are building an automated AI researcher, accelerating the broader economy, and giving every person on Earth a personal AGI. Two of the world's most consequential AI labs are now on a formal path to public markets within the same fortnight. For large corporate clients still treating AI vendor relationships as provisional or experimental, the IPO race signals that these are permanent infrastructure providers. The strategic question is no longer whether to commit — it is how to govern and negotiate the commitment.

## WHAT HAPPENED

- **OpenAI S-1 filing + "third phase" manifesto (8 June)**: Confidential S-1 filed. A potential September 2026 Wall Street debut has been reported by bankers, but OpenAI has not confirmed timing. The company is projecting a $14 billion loss for 2026 and does not expect profitability until 2029 — the same growth-at-scale economics that characterised Amazon Web Services and Facebook in their formative years. The Altman/Pachocki piece is worth reading in full: the section on distributed power — "a good AI future cannot be one where a small number of institutions control most of the capability and most of the upside" — is directly relevant for advising clients on AI vendor concentration risk.

- **ChatGPT "Dreaming" memory system now live (8 June)**: OpenAI rolled out a new persistent memory architecture to ChatGPT Plus and Pro users in the US. The system reviews past conversations during idle periods, extracts key facts, and stores them in a structured memory bank that self-updates over time — turning "you're going to Singapore in July" into "you went to Singapore in July" when the trip ends. Factual recall across sessions has moved from 41.5% (2024) to 82.8% (2026). A user-reviewable memory summary page is also shipping. Free tier support follows. Practical implication: ChatGPT is becoming meaningfully more useful for repeat-use advisory and research workflows without requiring manual context re-loading.

- **OpenAI Economic Research Exchange (8 June)**: New structured programme giving external economists structured, project-based access to OpenAI's data and teams to research AI's impact on workers, firms, and institutions. Application deadline July 5. Minor as a product launch but notable as a deliberate move: OpenAI is actively building an independent evidence base for "AI is net positive for labour" — useful framing for any client managing internal workforce change management around AI adoption.

## RETAIL & CONSUMER TECH

- **Best Buy + Meta — "Meta Lab @ Best Buy" (8 June)**: Best Buy is rolling out dedicated 900 sq ft Meta Lab spaces inside more than 50 US stores, launching this month with further rollout through summer and the holiday season. Each space features hands-on demos of Meta's full AI glasses and VR headset range, smart mirrors, and dedicated Meta Sales Specialists. Announced via BusinessWire. This is the first time Meta's AI hardware line has had a properly scaled branded retail floor presence. The more interesting angle for retail clients: this is a live bet on experiential, high-touch specialist retail as the conversion mechanism for an entirely new hardware category — a model with direct parallels to how Apple Stores sold the original iPhone. If AI glasses follow a similar adoption curve, expect competing brands and retailers to watch closely this holiday season.

Nothing else significant in retail startup fundraising today. Quiet 24 hours for seed and Series A announcements in the sector.

## PKM & KNOWLEDGE GRAPHS

- **MemPalace benchmark update (8 June)**: Fresh comparative benchmark data confirms MemPalace as the top-performing open-source AI memory system on LongMemEval, scoring 96.6% raw and 100% hybrid. Unlike summarisation-based memory tools, MemPalace stores verbatim conversation content and retrieves it via semantic search — structured as wings (people and projects), rooms (topics), and drawers (content). Fully local via ChromaDB and SQLite, zero API cost. The project launched in April and has since reached 55k GitHub stars. The new benchmark data — particularly the comparison against paid cloud memory systems — makes it a credible evaluation option for anyone building multi-session AI agents where context persistence matters and data sensitivity rules out cloud-hosted alternatives.
