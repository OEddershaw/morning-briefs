# Morning Brief — 2026-06-04

## THE ONE THING

DeepSeek is closing in on a $7.4 billion maiden fundraise at a valuation of up to $59 billion, according to Bloomberg and multiple sources reporting on 3–4 June. Tencent and CATL are leading, with China's National Artificial Intelligence Industry Investment Fund participating — direct state endorsement. The founder is committing 20 billion yuan of his own capital; term sheets are being signed this week. The significance is structural: DeepSeek built its global reputation as a self-funded lab that matched GPT-class performance at a fraction of the cost. That era ends with this round. It becomes an institutionalised, state-backed Chinese AI champion. For clients building AI strategies, the question shifts from model capability comparisons to vendor risk, IP exposure, and what Beijing-endorsed AI infrastructure means for long-term supply chain positioning.

## WHAT HAPPENED

- **AlphaSense raises $350M at $7.5B, launches SuperAnalyst (3 June)**: The AI market intelligence platform crossed $600M ARR in Q1 2026 and nearly doubled its valuation. Accenture invested and becomes its first strategic channel partner. The more relevant announcement: SuperAnalyst, an always-on AI agent that runs research workflows, builds custom dashboards, tracks developments, and delivers decision-ready intelligence autonomously — all grounded in AlphaSense's premium licensed content (earnings calls, broker research, filings). The round brings in D. E. Shaw Ventures and Pinegrove alongside existing backers CapitalG, Goldman Sachs Alternatives, and Viking Global. Relevant both as a competitive signal for advisory firms and as a potential client-facing tool.

- **Anthropic formalises the Claude partner ecosystem (3 June)**: New Services Track creates three certified tiers for consulting and services firms — Select (10 certified practitioners, 2 deployed clients), Preferred (100 practitioners, 15 clients), and Global Premier (1,000 practitioners, 100 deployed clients across 3 regions). 40,000+ firms have applied since March; 10,000+ consultants hold Claude certifications. A new Partner Hub lets enterprise buyers find and evaluate qualified implementation partners. Relevant for clients who want structured help building Claude-based products and need a way to evaluate vendor claims rather than relying on self-reported expertise.

## RETAIL & CONSUMER TECH

- **Forage — $40M Series B (3 June)**: Forage builds the payment infrastructure that enables retailers to accept government benefits — SNAP, WIC, HSA/FSA — online and via delivery platforms. Already embedded in 100,000+ US stores across all 50 states, including Dollar General, Gopuff, Save A Lot, DoorDash, and Uber Eats. Round led by Mouro Capital with PayPal Ventures, Intuit Ventures, Pivotal Ventures, and FJ Labs, at a $225M valuation. The capital funds two tracks in parallel: deeper infrastructure expansion and a consumer-facing savings app targeting one million benefit-eligible families by end of 2026. For US grocery and mass-market retail clients, Forage is now the credible infrastructure layer for digital government payments — a segment growing as more benefit programmes expand online eligibility and as delivery platforms become primary grocery channels for low-income shoppers.

Nothing else significant in the last 24 hours.

## COMPETITIVE WATCH

- **AlphaSense + Accenture channel partnership**: Accenture's strategic investment and channel agreement puts AlphaSense's AI market intelligence layer into Accenture's consulting delivery at scale. Accenture is now formally positioned to embed AI-powered market and competitive research into corporate strategy engagements via a productised platform. Worth watching as a signal of how the larger consultancies are moving to augment advisory work with always-on intelligence agents — and how that changes the value proposition of manual landscape and vendor research.
